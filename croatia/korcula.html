Korčula

Korčula, often referred to as "Little Dubrovnik," is a gem of an island located in the Adriatic Sea, just off the Dalmatian coast. Known for its dense forests, Korčula is one of the greenest islands in the Adriatic, earning it the moniker "The Emerald Isle."

The island's main town, also named Korčula, is a beautifully preserved medieval walled city. Its herringbone pattern of narrow streets and alleys is designed to provide shelter from the sun and the prevailing winds. The town is also famous as the alleged birthplace of the renowned explorer Marco Polo.

Korčula offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters to its rich winemaking tradition and the energetic Moreška sword dance, Korčula provides a diverse range of experiences for every traveler.

Key Attractions:

  • Korčula Old Town: Explore the medieval architecture and visit the alleged house of Marco Polo.
  • Beaches: Relax on beautiful beaches like Pupnatska Luka, Vela Przina, and Banje Beach.
  • Wineries: Taste local wines, especially the white wine Grk, unique to Korčula.
  • Moreška Sword Dance: Watch this traditional dance performance, a symbol of Korčula's cultural heritage.
  • St. Mark's Cathedral: Admire the blend of Gothic and Renaissance styles in this 15th-century cathedral.
